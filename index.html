Hear their cry, don't let them die...

Why Do They Matter?

Blue whales are on the brink of extinction, and hence, must be protected, regardless of what anyone says. Not only are they majestic creatures that do not deserve to be taken away from their own home, but they also contribute to humanity. Saving the whales in some manner would save humans and the world.

Whale biologists think that only a fourth of the planet's population remains with blue whales at as little as 3% of their previous numbers. Restoring these incredible sea mammals to their pre-whaling numbers could prove vital to reversing climate change. The animals accumulate carbon in their bodies throughout their life and then sink to the bottom of the ocean sequestering huge amounts of CO2. A great whale, for example, can take nearly 30000 kg of CO2 out of the atmosphere for centuries compared to the around 15 kg captured by a tree each year.

It's not just what they store in their bodies, however. The waste they produce is brought to the surface by their vertical movement and feeds organisms called phytoplankton in a unique system that no other animal can reproduce. Generating 50% of all oxygen and capturing 40% of CO2 produced, these vital organisms rely on whales to provide nutrients and maintain their numbers. If the number of whales was restored to around 5 million this would significantly increase phytoplankton numbers.


What Can You Do To Help?

Clearly, saving whales is a must, as they are largely important to our ecosystem. And as inhabitants of the same planet, it's our duty to save these gentle creatures. So how can the average person help save the blue whales?
